Mastering Throws, Holds, and Pins. Judo techniques are divided into standing techniques (Tachi-waza) and groundwork (Ne-waza). Practitioners focus on throws, joint locks, and pins to outmaneuver opponents, with an emphasis on balance, timing, and leverage.

Comprehensive Judo Training. Our Judo training programs cater to practitioners of all skill levels, from beginners to advanced competitors. The curriculum includes physical conditioning, technique drills, and sparring sessions (Randori), ensuring a well-rounded development for each judoka.

Rules and Etiquette in Judo Competitions. Judo competitions are governed by the International Judo Federation (IJF). Matches are fought on a tatami mat, where participants score points through throws, holds, or submissions. Competitors must demonstrate respect and sportsmanship at all times, following traditional bowing protocols.

The Path of Progress in Judo. The Judo belt system signifies a practitioner’s rank and level of skill. Beginners start as white belts and advance through yellow, green, blue, and brown belts. Black belts represent mastery, with higher degrees (dan) awarded for significant accomplishments and contributions to the art.
